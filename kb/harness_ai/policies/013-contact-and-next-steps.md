# Contact and Next Steps

The best next step is to define a small first use case. HarnessAI prefers a bounded pilot that can be built, tested, observed, and improved before expanding.

A useful first conversation should identify the organization, the audience, the workflow problem, the common questions people ask, the source documents available, and any privacy or approval concerns.

For CommPilot, the next step is to choose a dedicated mailbox, collect approved source material, define the categories of questions the system should answer, and define what it should refuse or escalate.

For a white-labeled AnythingLLM deployment, the next step is to identify the document collection, expected users, model preferences, hosting needs, and access-control expectations.

For AI consulting, the next step is to choose one workflow that is repetitive, costly, confusing, or hard to scale.

For synthetic data, the next step is to define the schema, the purpose of the data, the realism requirements, and how the synthetic data will be reviewed.

Visitors who are unsure where to start can ask the HarnessAI app: What is the smallest AI pilot my organization should try first?
