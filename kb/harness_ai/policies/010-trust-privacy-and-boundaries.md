# Trust, Privacy, and Boundaries

HarnessAI systems should be deployed with clear data boundaries and human oversight. The goal is practical assistance, not uncontrolled automation.

For CommPilot, HarnessAI recommends using a dedicated mailbox instead of an organization's primary mailbox. This keeps the pilot safer, easier to monitor, and easier to isolate from sensitive communication.

HarnessAI prefers narrow first deployments that answer approved categories of routine questions. The system should know when to say it does not know and when to refer a question to a person.

Organizations should decide what information can be included in the knowledge base, what information should be excluded, who can review the logs, and who is responsible for updating source material.

HarnessAI does not recommend using a basic public-facing AI front desk for emergency response, legal advice, medical advice, financial advice, disciplinary matters, confidential records, or final decisions about rights, benefits, eligibility, or compliance.

Model and vendor transparency matter. Organizations should know what model providers are being used, where inference is happening, and what data may be sent to an external service.

A useful AI system should improve consistency and response time while preserving a clear path to human judgment.
